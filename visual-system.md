# Visual System

## Logo System

- Wordmark: primary signature for websites, proposals, documents, and presentations.
- Icon: compact mark for avatars, app icons, social profiles, and square placements.
- Favicon: browser and tiny-surface version of the icon.
- Preferred presentation: dusty amethyst on warm cream, or transparent when the background already supports the mark.

## Typography

- Display font: [Source Serif 4](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Serif+4), used in semibold and bold for headlines only.
- Body and UI font: [IBM Plex Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/IBM+Plex+Mono), used in regular and medium for body text, labels, and interface copy.

Recommended scale:

| Size | Use |
| --- | --- |
| 48px | Hero headline |
| 32px | Page title |
| 24px | Section heading |
| 20px | Subheading |
| 16px | Body text |
| 14px | Caption and helper text |
| 12px | Legal or footnote text |

## Visual Direction

- Warm, tactile, editorial
- Architectural spacing and generous negative space
- Serif headlines with monospace body copy
- Quiet authority instead of startup flash
- Material, grounded surfaces instead of glossy effects

## Do

- Use the wordmark whenever there is room for the full brand name.
- Keep calm contrast and generous spacing around the logo.
- Let the cream background and warm near-black text do most of the work.
- Treat the icon as a compact signature, not a decorative pattern.

## Avoid

- Pure black backgrounds as the default brand look
- Neon greens, candy gradients, or glassmorphism
- Crowded layouts or hyperactive motion
- Mixing serif and monospace within the same hierarchy level
